Tire Pressure Tuner

Tire pressures should generally be tuned to achieve a hot pressure of 30–34 PSI on asphalt.
Aim for an optimal target of 32–33 PSI for maximum grip.
Start cold at 28–30 PSI and adjust based on telemetry.

Guidelines
  • Optimal Hot: 30–34 PSI (after driving)
  • Road/Racing: 28–30 PSI (Cold)
  • Dirt/Rally: 25–30 PSI
  • Cross Country: ~24 PSI
Tuning Logic
  • Less Pressure: More grip, less responsive
  • More Pressure: Less grip, sharper handling
  • Understeer Fix: Decrease Front / Increase Rear
  • Oversteer Fix: Increase Front / Decrease Rear
Telemetry Pro-Tip

Monitor tire heat in the telemetry overlay.
Aim for 2/3 to full orange/yellow coloring during heavy cornering to ensure the tire is working at its peak temperature.

Note: Default pressures are often too high. Starting with a lower, equal setting across all tires is usually the best first step.
